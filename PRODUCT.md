# Supply Pro — Product Brief

## What it is
Supply Pro is an early-warning system for professional kitchens. It detects supplier issues before the delivery truck arrives and presents affected products, reasons, and ready alternatives in one alert. Chefs resolve supply problems in under 3 minutes without phone calls or WhatsApp.

## Register
brand

## Users

**Primary: Head chefs and sous chefs in professional kitchens**
- High-stress environment — every second during service counts
- Used to chaos but deeply frustrated by preventable chaos (late deliveries, wrong items)
- Not tech-averse, but UI must be instant and obvious — no learning curve tolerated
- Mobile-first: they check their phone between prep tasks, often with wet or busy hands

**Secondary: Suppliers and supplier reps**
- Need to communicate issues clearly and quickly to multiple kitchen clients
- Currently do this via WhatsApp or phone — friction on both sides

## Brand

**Tone:** Calm authority. Not cheerful. Not corporate. Like a trusted colleague who has seen everything and doesn't panic.

**Anti-references (do not look like these):**
- Generic SaaS blue-button-on-white (Notion, Linear aesthetic)
- Food delivery apps (Deliveroo, Uber Eats — consumer, not professional)
- Restaurant booking apps (OpenTable — wrong audience)
- Loud startup marketing (animated gradients everywhere, floating 3D blobs)

**References (spirit, not literal copy):**
- Stripe — precision, seriousness, craft in the details
- Vercel — dark/green accent, technical confidence
- Literal kitchen tools — heavy, precise, built to last

## Positioning
"Know before the truck arrives."

Built from 10 expert interviews across 5 countries (Switzerland, France, Spain, Germany, Uruguay). Every design decision traces to a real conversation with a working chef.

## Strategic principles
1. Speed over features — the value is the warning arriving in time, not the feature count
2. One decision per alert — no dashboards, no noise, one clear action
3. Research-grounded — credibility comes from field work, not from claiming to be "the future of food"
4. Not consumer, not enterprise — professional tools for skilled tradespeople
